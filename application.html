Mathematical Application of Matrices

LU decomposition breaks down a square matrix into the product of two triangular matrices: a lower triangular matrix (L) and an upper triangular matrix (U). This decomposition is used to solve systems of linear equations, calculate matrix inverses, and find determinants.

Eigenvalue decomposition is a process that breaks down a square matrix into its constituent eigenvalues and eigenvectors, essentially representing it in terms of its core components. This decomposition is widely used across various fields like machine learning, physics, and data science. It's particularly valuable in tasks like dimensionality reduction, where it helps identify the most important features or directions in a dataset.



View EigenValue Decomposition

QR decomposition breaks down a matrix into two simpler matrices: an orthogonal matrix (Q) and an upper triangular matrix (R). This technique is used in various applications, including solving linear systems of equations, finding eigenvalues and eigenvectors of a matrix, and solving least squares problems.



View QR Decomposition

Cholesky decomposition is a method in linear algebra that breaks down a positive-definite Hermitian matrix into the product of a lower triangular matrix and its conjugate transpose. This process is computationally efficient, particularly for solving systems of linear equations and in applications involving kernel inversion. It's often used in statistics, machine learning, and other fields where working with covariance matrices or similar structures is common.
